Artificial Intelligence will bring money! India can become a $1 trillion digital economy by 2028

10/08/2024 11:36

Minister of State for Electronics and IT Rajiv Chandrashekhar says that India's digital economy is growing at the rate of 2.8 percent annually and it can reach 1 trillion dollars by 2027-28. He said that the value of IndiaAI mission will also double from Rs 10000 crore to Rs 20000 crore. Let us know its complete details.

Target extended by one year

Union Minister Chandrashekhar says that the government had earlier hoped that India's digital economy would reach the figure of 1 trillion dollars only by 2026-27. But, the target has been extended by one year due to unforeseen challenges like the Covid-19 pandemic.

The Union Minister said, 'Prime Minister Narendra Modi is focusing a lot on digitalization. This is the reason why the digital economy is growing at the rate of 2.8 percent. We are already the fastest growing digital economy in the world.

Chandrashekhar was speaking at a 'Special Contact Campaign' at the official residence of Union Minister Hardeep Singh Puri on Monday evening. About 300 IT, startup and technology industry celebrities participated in the event.

Seed fund of Rs 1 lakh crore

Minister of State for Electronics and IT Chandrashekhar said that the Prime Minister has already approved a seed fund of Rs 1 lakh crore to promote AI. This will promote innovation in the digital sector. For example, if someone has created technology, then IndiaAI Mission will be used for its research and development. Its total value will be Rs 20,000 crore.

This is the aim of the government

The Union Minister said that the aim of our government is to take young Indians forward so that they can compete at the global level. He said that the tech industry wants this pace of growth to continue and our focus is also on this thing.

He said that Prime Minister Modi had started the Digital India Mission in 2015. Under this, every Indian will have an internet connection and will be fully digitally empowered.
